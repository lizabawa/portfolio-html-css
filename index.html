Career

My career has been a journey. I feel that I've lived a hundred lifetimes with all the different paths I have tried out, and it has all led me to developing. I graduated in 2016 with a degree in Environmental Health. No idea what that is huh? Basically, I was a health inspector. The only reason I got the degree was because I have never been "book smart", so naturally I gravitated to the easiest degree I could find. I was an inspector for a handful of years and then pivoted.

My next role was in a customer service/accounts payable type role remotely. I exceled in the quota/bonus-based environment, and my goal initially was to at some point move up into HR or tech, but those opportunities unfortunately never arose for me.

Eventually an external role to be a recruiter came up, and I took because that was in the HR world I thought I wanted to pivot into. Unfortunately, burnout got the best of me and I resigned to become a receptionist at an eye doctor.

A receptionist is an easy and unchallenging role, and I found that I craved to use the analytical part of my brain. Due to my time as a recruiter, I saw how much developers could make and that the open roles were always remote. I knew I enjoyed remote work from my time in my accounts payable role, and I knew I wanted a job that challenges my critical thinking skills.

That's how I ended up as a developer. For the first time I believe I've found a career I enjoy doing. My career goal now is to land my first developer job!
